Graph complexity — distinct degree types

Each pixel in the 10×10 downsampled frame is a node. An edge is drawn between pixels with similarity > 0.5 (globally normalised LAB distance). The count of distinct node degrees measures structural variety in the mixing pattern. Hypothesis: peaks at intermediate mixing when thin lamellae create the most varied connectivity.

Boltzmann mixing entropy (exact, normalised)

Computed directly from the 64×64 concentration field: S = ⟨−[x ln x + (1−x) ln(1−x)]⟩ / ln 2. No colour-approximation involved. S = 0 when fluids are fully separated; S = 1 when every site is at exactly 50/50. Expected to rise monotonically (second law of thermodynamics).

Complexity vs Boltzmann entropy

Each point is one simulation step. Colour runs from blue (early) to orange (late), showing the trajectory through entropy–complexity space. A rightward arc that peaks then falls would confirm that complexity is maximised at intermediate entropy — the edge-of-chaos signature.
